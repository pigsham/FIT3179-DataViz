Global Overview

Percentage of Tourism GDP by Countries

The percentage of tourism GDP data reveals fluctuations due to economic changes and the COVID-19 pandemic. Countries heavily reliant on tourism, like Aruba, Malta, and Antigua and Barbuda, consistently show high percentages (over 10%). A sharp decline occurred in 2020 for many nations, especially Mexico, Thailand, and Vietnam, due to travel restrictions. Recovery is visible, though some countries still lag behind pre-pandemic levels.

Tourist Arrivals Over the Years

International tourist arrivals showed steady growth across all regions from 2010 to 2019, with Europe leading in numbers. However, the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 caused a dramatic drop, especially in Europe, East Asia, and the Americas. For example, Europe saw a fall from 878 million in 2019 to 325 million in 2020. While there was a partial recovery in 2021, tourist arrivals remained significantly below pre-pandemic levels, highlighting the ongoing impact of the pandemic on global tourism.

Travel Benefits

Traveling offers numerous benefits that contribute to both personal growth and mental well-being. It broadens perspectives, fosters creativity, and strengthens social connections by exposing individuals to new cultures and experiences.

Additionally, it reduces stress, boosts happiness, and enhances problem-solving skills. These benefits contribute to improved cognitive flexibility and adaptability, making travel an essential activity for mental health and personal development.

Accommodation Apps Revenue

Booking leads in revenue, especially in 2023, due to its global reach and seamless booking experience.

TripAdvisor, focusing more on reviews, consistently shows the smallest revenue.

While Booking excels in direct bookings, TripAdvisor relies on referrals. Despite this, both apps provide easy access to accommodations, simplifying travel planning for users.
